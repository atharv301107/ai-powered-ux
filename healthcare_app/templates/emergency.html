Critical First Aid Guidelines

🫀
Cardiac Arrest & Severe Chest Pain

1. Call the emergency helpline instantly.
2. Have the patient sit upright and rest quietly to reduce heart load.
3. Loosen tight clothing around the neck.
4. If conscious, let them chew an Aspirin (75mg–300mg) if available.
5. If unconscious and not breathing, start Cardio-Pulmonary Resuscitation (CPR) immediately.

🩸
Severe Uncontrolled Bleeding

1. Elevate the wounded area above the level of the heart if possible.
2. Apply firm, direct pressure on the wound using a clean sterile dressing or cloth.
3. Do NOT remove any embedded objects; apply padding around them and secure with a bandage.
4. Keep pressing until assistance arrives. Do not lift dressing to check if bleeding stopped.

🌬️
Acute Breathing Difficulty

1. Assist the patient in sitting in an upright position (never flat).
2. Assist with administering their emergency inhaler or nebulizer if prescribed.
3. Loosen restrictive clothing. Ensure room has adequate ventilation.
4. Keep the patient calm. Panic increases oxygen demand.

🧠
Stroke Recognition (F.A.S.T.)

Recognize stroke signs immediately using the FAST clinical method:
• F (Face): Ask the person to smile. Does one side of the face droop?
• A (Arm): Ask them to raise both arms. Does one arm drift downward?
• S (Speech): Ask them to repeat a simple sentence. Is speech slurred?
• T (Time): If you observe any of these symptoms, call emergency services immediately.
